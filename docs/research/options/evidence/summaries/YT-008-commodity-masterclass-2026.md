# YT-008 — Commodity Trading Masterclass 2026

**Status:** Draft Research  
**Source:** [FREE Commodity Trading Masterclass 2026](https://youtu.be/d9VI5LVWGcg)  
**Publisher:** Dhan and Commodities by Dhan  
**Review status:** Reviewed

## Observation

The source combines commodity-market orientation with position sizing, event awareness, staggered position management, and an example of adding long wings to limit short-option risk.

## Evidence

- Commodity volatility and margin are discussed as product-dependent.
- Producers and consumers are described as material commodity-market participants.
- Smaller contract units are presented as enabling partial entries and exits.
- Crude inventory, Natural Gas data, payroll data, and central-bank decisions are highlighted as volatility events.
- Buying protective wings is demonstrated as a way to convert an uncapped short structure into defined risk.

## Possible Interpretation

Event exposure, position granularity, and bounded versus unbounded loss deserve separate validation. The structural concept of protective wings can be retained without the source's market calls or numerical examples.

## Open Questions

- How do defined-risk structures behave after actual spreads, margin, and exit execution?
- Which event effects are stable across products and regimes?
- Does position granularity materially improve stress governance?

## Not Retained

- Product risk hierarchies presented as universal.
- Fixed timing claims about when liquidity or opportunity is best.
- Margin examples, claimed Gold ranges, payoff figures, or performance implications.
- Any demonstrated structure as a recommendation.
