# Research Hypotheses

**Status:** Draft Research  
**Authority:** None

The hypotheses below define future evidence work. They intentionally avoid production thresholds, signals, strike rules, or implementation.

| ID | Hypothesis | Evidence that could challenge it | Minimum research need |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| HYP-001 | Premium selling outcomes depend partly on future realized movement relative to movement priced by options. | Stable outcomes unrelated to implied/realized relationships | Synchronized option-chain and underlying histories |
| HYP-002 | Near-expiry theta benefits can be overwhelmed by concentrated gamma and execution risk. | Robust risk-adjusted outcomes after gaps, spreads, and assignment | Intraday expiry replay with Greeks and executable quotes |
| HYP-003 | Defined-risk wings reduce tail loss but may materially alter expectancy, liquidity, and capital use. | No meaningful stress benefit after costs | Matched defined-risk and naked scenario analysis |
| HYP-004 | COMEX context sometimes adds information to MCX metals decisions after currency and session effects are controlled. | No incremental value or unstable lead-lag relationships | Timestamp-aligned COMEX, MCX, and USDINR evidence |
| HYP-005 | Short-horizon option buying requires sufficiently timely movement to overcome decay and trading friction. | Buying outcomes insensitive to movement speed or costs | Underlying, option quote, IV, and fill-level replay |
| HYP-006 | Buying after volatility expansion can underperform despite correct direction because of volatility contraction. | Direction explains outcomes without IV contribution | Event and non-event samples with IV history |
| HYP-007 | Apparent option opportunities disappear more often in contracts with weak depth or wide spreads. | Similar executable outcomes across liquidity conditions | Bid, ask, depth, trade, and rejected-order evidence |
| HYP-008 | Rolling outcomes cannot be attributed correctly without preserving the reason and exposure change for every adjustment. | Opening structure alone explains outcomes consistently | Complete position lifecycle and intent tagging |
| HYP-009 | Delta neutrality alone can conceal material gamma, vega, skew, and gap exposure. | Neutral-delta positions show stable stress behavior regardless of other Greeks | Position- and leg-level Greek histories |
| HYP-010 | Event windows require separate treatment from ordinary volatility regimes. | No material difference in gaps, IV, spread, or adjustment outcomes | Event calendar aligned to option and position histories |
| HYP-011 | Account-level outcomes differ partly because of execution and governance, not only market direction. | Normalized accounts show no process-related differences | Comparable Account #1 and #2 ledgers and constraints |
| HYP-012 | A futures leg's stated intent affects valid attribution of a combined options position. | Attribution remains unchanged across hedge, adjustment, and directional intent | Contemporaneous intent and linkage evidence |

Recording a hypothesis does not endorse it.
