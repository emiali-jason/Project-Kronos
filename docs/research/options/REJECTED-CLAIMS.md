# Rejected Claims

**Status:** Draft Research  
**Meaning of rejected:** Not accepted as a KRONOS conclusion, rule, threshold, or validated edge. Rejection here does not assert that every claim is universally false.

| ID | Claim not retained | Reason |
| --- | --- | --- |
| RC-001 | High premium or high implied volatility automatically makes selling attractive. | Premium may reflect genuine event, movement, skew, liquidity, or gap risk. |
| RC-002 | Delta is an exact expiry probability or a complete measure of strike safety. | Delta is a changing model sensitivity and omits loss severity, path, volatility, and execution. |
| RC-003 | An out-of-the-money strike is inherently safe. | Moneyness and Greeks change as price, time, and volatility change. |
| RC-004 | Near-expiry theta is free or low-risk income. | Gamma, gaps, spreads, assignment, and devolvement can dominate decay. |
| RC-005 | A high win rate or a claimed 60%–65% success probability proves profitability. | Win rate omits loss distribution, costs, tail risk, and sample definition. |
| RC-006 | Markets remain sideways a fixed percentage of the time in a way that guarantees seller advantage. | Regime, instrument, horizon, and measurement are unspecified. |
| RC-007 | Fixed India VIX bands determine whether ATM or OTM options should be sold. | Playlist thresholds were personal guidance, not validated cross-market evidence. |
| RC-008 | Fixed Gold/Silver-ratio levels produce reliable relative-value trades. | Historical relationship, regime stability, and implementation were not validated. |
| RC-009 | Capturing 70%–75% of premium is an approved close-and-roll rule. | This is a user-reported heuristic without normalized evidence. |
| RC-010 | A fixed opening time, premium value, moving average, or indicator setting establishes an option-buying edge. | Buying videos provide educational setups, not independent validation. |
| RC-011 | Gold Mini, Silver Micro, or any other contract is universally the best choice. | Liquidity, specifications, costs, capital, and availability vary over time. |
| RC-012 | Technical analysis is reliable because globally traded commodities cannot be manipulated. | This is an unsupported generalization. |
| RC-013 | The masterclass's numerical Gold range or other rule of thumb is reusable. | The source example is context-specific and unvalidated. |
| RC-014 | The expiry session's strikes, range, exit levels, probability of profit, or payoff figures are reusable. | They were tied to one option-chain snapshot and were not independently reconstructed. |
| RC-015 | A live example, personal profit claim, or educational payoff proves repeatable performance. | No complete dataset, costs, sample, or independent audit was supplied. |
| RC-016 | Delta-neutral exposure is risk-neutral exposure. | Gamma, vega, skew, convexity, liquidity, and gaps may remain material. |

Rejected claims may be revisited only through new evidence; they must not silently re-enter as assumptions.
