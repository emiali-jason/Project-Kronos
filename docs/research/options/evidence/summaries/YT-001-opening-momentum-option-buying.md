# YT-001 — Opening-Momentum Option Buying

**Status:** Draft Research  
**Source:** [Simple 9:30 AM Nifty Option Buying Strategy for Beginners and Busy Professionals](https://youtu.be/uvut8SRcFGY)  
**Review status:** Reviewed through the accepted buying-video working note

## Observation

The source emphasizes rapid confirmation of an opening directional idea, responsive option contracts, and risk decisions made before entry.

## Evidence

- Delta and moneyness are used to discuss contract responsiveness.
- The expected move is presented as needing to appear promptly for a short-horizon buyer.
- Continued holding without momentum is associated with decay and reversal exposure.
- Maximum loss, profit exit, and time-based invalidation are discussed as pre-trade decisions.
- Paper validation is encouraged before relying on presentation confidence.

## Possible Interpretation

Movement speed, option responsiveness, time exposure, and execution friction may need to be evaluated together.

## Open Questions

- Do the observations persist outside the opening period?
- What remains after spreads, slippage, fees, and rejected orders?
- How could time-based evidence be tested without importing the source's rule?

## Not Retained

The fixed opening time, premium references, entries, exits, and performance claims are not KRONOS rules.
