# YT-003 — Option-Buying Foundations and Multi-Timeframe Momentum

**Status:** Draft Research  
**Source:** [The Ultimate 1-Hour Masterclass on Option Buying Strategies](https://youtu.be/HAUSZx-hYdY)  
**Review status:** Reviewed through the accepted buying-video working note

## Observation

The source frames buying as a combination of underlying context, movement quality, option behavior, and execution conditions.

## Evidence

- Calls, puts, strikes, expiries, and the option chain are treated as foundations.
- Delta explains responsiveness, theta the cost of time, and IV premium expansion or contraction.
- Higher and lower timeframes are assigned different analytical roles.
- Liquidity and bid-ask spread are treated as execution considerations.
- Understanding an indicator's purpose is distinguished from copying settings.

## Possible Interpretation

Underlying context, option-chain characteristics, and execution quality may need separate evidence before their relationships are tested.

## Open Questions

- Which option-chain fields are necessary for reproducible research?
- How should event-related IV expansion and contraction be studied?
- Which examples survive across instruments, expiries, and regimes?

## Not Retained

Indicators, historical demonstrations, fixed rules, and profit examples are not evidence of a persistent edge.
