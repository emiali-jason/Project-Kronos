# Convergence Model — C0 to C5

## Purpose

Convergence records how independently and directly the registered evidence supports an existing Extracted Principle. It does not measure popularity, source count, truth, architectural relevance, validation status, or acceptance.

The model is proposition-specific. A source may support different propositions at different convergence levels.

## Levels

| Level | Name | Minimum interpretation |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `C0` | Unmapped | No registered source-to-proposition support is recorded, or available material is too incomplete to establish support. |
| `C1` | Single-source signal | One reviewed source supplies direct or illustrative support. The proposition remains dependent on that source or evidence lineage. |
| `C2` | Repeated signal | Two or more sources support or illustrate the proposition, but independence, method diversity, or direct empirical support is not established. |
| `C3` | Independent convergence | At least two demonstrably independent evidence streams support the proposition using materially distinct sources, datasets, or methods; conflicts and scope limits are recorded. |
| `C4` | Triangulated convergence | Multiple independent evidence families support the proposition, including at least one direct, substantially transparent, and reproducible or authoritative evidence stream; material conflicts and disconfirming evidence have been assessed. |
| `C5` | Validated convergence | Purpose-designed independent validation or replication directly tests the proposition with an auditable method, results, scope, failures, and conflict review. |

## Assignment Rules

1. Assign convergence to the exact Extracted Principle, never to an entire source, topic, or Architecture Candidate.
2. Source count alone cannot determine a level.
3. Sources sharing an author, event, dataset, method lineage, or underlying information must not be treated as independent without evidence.
4. If independence is not established, convergence cannot exceed `C2`.
5. `C3` requires demonstrated independence and material evidence diversity, not merely books plus videos.
6. `C4` requires direct evidence with substantial method transparency and reproducibility or authoritative formal support; practitioner repetition alone is insufficient.
7. `C5` requires a documented independent validation or replication record. A consensus summary alone cannot produce `C5`.
8. Conflicting evidence must be recorded. A conflict does not automatically lower the level, but unresolved material conflicts prevent `C4` or `C5`.
9. Provisional or substantively unreviewed sources may be mapped for traceability but cannot raise a proposition above `C1` by themselves.
10. Raising or lowering convergence requires a dated rationale and updated source traceability.

## Architectural Boundary

No convergence level accepts an Extracted Principle, approves an Architecture Candidate, creates architecture, or authorises implementation. `C5` means validated evidence convergence only; the separate research lifecycle, architecture review, and explicit approval requirements still apply.
