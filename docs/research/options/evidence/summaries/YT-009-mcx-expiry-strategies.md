## Observation

The useful Options material is a Gold expiry case study: sell near-ATM premium for rapid decay, react when the assumed range fails, and use long wings to define loss.

## Evidence

- The session cited an ATM IV, remaining time value, a next-morning price range, and short call/put legs at one strike.
- It proposed closing the threatened short leg after specified underlying breaches and retaining the opposite side with a cost-oriented stop.
- It demonstrated an iron-fly-style structure with a short call and put plus lower put and upper call wings.
- It reported net premium, maximum risk, potential reward, and probability of profit from the contemporaneous option chain.
- The speakers warned that Greeks and risk management must be understood.

## Possible Interpretation

Expiry selling combines decay with concentrated short-gamma, gap, liquidity, and settlement risk. Closing one threatened leg also transforms a nominally neutral position into directional exposure. Protective wings bound terminal loss but do not remove execution risk.
