## Observation

The note preserves a user-reported MCX selling practice centered on rolling positions and governance under stress, not merely opening a short strangle.

## Evidence

- Study orientation: approximately 90% selling and 10% buying.
- Current metals focus: GoldM, SilverM, and Copper; future energy study: Crude Oil and Natural Gas.
- Reported practice: rolling short strangles, a 70%–75% premium-capture reference, and close-and-roll actions.
- Reported context: COMEX-first / MCX-confirmation, CPR, and price action.
- Reported exposure orientation: delta-neutral default, temporary structure-aware delta, and an overnight move toward neutrality.
- Futures intent should remain identifiable as hedge, adjustment, or directional trade.
- Account #1 and Account #2 remain separate evidence streams without accessible normalized ledgers.
- January 29–30, 2026 is identified as a metals stress episode requiring reconstruction.
- The central unresolved problem is position governance under stress.

## Possible Interpretation

Research should preserve the complete chronology of observations, decisions, orders, fills, exposures, costs, and intent. Opening payoff or account-level P&L alone cannot attribute governance quality.

## Open Questions

- Can the accounts and stress episode be reconstructed from contemporaneous records?
- How were rolls, temporary delta, futures overlays, and overnight exposure actually decided?
- Which outcomes came from the market, execution constraints, or governance choices?
- Does the reported premium-capture heuristic survive normalization across instruments and regimes?

## Not Retained

The allocation split, premium-capture range, rolling style, neutrality preference, COMEX sequence, and checklist placeholder are observations—not approved requirements, thresholds, rules, ownership, or architecture.
