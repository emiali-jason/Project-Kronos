# YT-007 — Gold & Silver Trading after 5 PM

**Status:** Draft Research  
**Source:** [Gold & Silver Trading after 5 PM! | Commodity Series #3](https://youtu.be/bqchpq1n1Ec)  
**Publisher:** marketfeed  
**Review status:** Reviewed

## Observation

The video introduces MCX Gold and Silver contract variants, international price references, currency conversion, liquidity differences, extended trading, and common macro drivers.

## Evidence

- Gold and Silver are available in several contract sizes with different capital and liquidity characteristics.
- International precious-metal prices and USDINR are described as contributors to Indian prices.
- Gold is linked to safe-haven, inflation, interest-rate, currency, and geopolitical narratives; Silver also has industrial demand.
- The source warns that some smaller contracts and the bullion index may have weaker liquidity.
- Later global-market participation is presented as relevant to evening trading activity.

## Possible Interpretation

Instrument choice should be researched using current specifications and executable liquidity, while cross-market studies should account for currency and session overlap.

## Open Questions

- Which current contracts have reliable option-chain depth and tight spreads?
- How much explanatory value comes from global prices versus USDINR and local basis?
- How do liquidity and volatility vary through the MCX session?

## Not Retained

- A universal preference for Gold Mini or Silver Micro.
- Fixed expiry conventions or trading hours without current verification.
- Gold/Silver-ratio thresholds.
- The claim that globally integrated markets make technical analysis reliably effective.
